1. 
A. PURPOSES AND LIMITATIONS 
2 
As Plaintiff Activision Publishing, Inc. (“Plaintiff”) has represented, discovery 
3  in this action is likely to involve production of confidential, proprietary, or private 
4  information for which special protection from public disclosure and from use for any 
5  purpose other than prosecuting this litigation may be warranted.  Accordingly, this 
6  Court enters  the following Protective Order.   This Order does not confer blanket 
7  protections on all disclosures or responses to discovery.  The protection it affords from 
8  public disclosure and use extends only to the limited information or items that are 
9  entitled to confidential treatment under the applicable legal principles.  Further, as set 
10  forth in Section 12.3, below, this Protective Order does not entitle the parties to file 
11  confidential information under seal.  Rather, when the parties seek permission from 
12  the court to file material under seal, the parties must comply with Civil Local Rule 
13  79-5  and with any pertinent orders of the assigned District Judge and Magistrate 
14  Judge. 
15 
B. GOOD CAUSE STATEMENT 
16 
In light of the nature of the claims and allegations in this case and Plaintiff’s 
17  representation that discovery in this case will involve the production of confidential 
18  records, and in order to expedite the flow of information, to facilitate the prompt 
19  resolution of disputes over confidentiality of discovery materials, to adequately 
20  protect information the parties are entitled to keep confidential, to ensure that the 
21  parties are permitted reasonable necessary uses of such material in connection with 
22  this action, to address their handling of such material at the end of the litigation, and 
23  to serve the ends of justice, a protective order for such information is justified in this 
24  matter.  It is Plaintiff’s intent that any information/documents shall not be 
25  designated as confidential without a good faith belief that such information/ 
26  documents have been maintained in a confidential, non-public manner, and that 
27  there is good cause or a compelling reason why it should not be part of the public 
28  record of this case.
