NOTICE OF EX PARTE APPLICATION 
2
PLEASE TAKE NOTICE that Plaintiff Activision Publishing, Inc. 
3 (“Plaintiff”) hereby applies ex parte pursuant to Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 
4 26(d) for the Court to enter a protective order (the “Protective Order”) in the 
5 above-captioned case in order to clarify the parameters of confidentiality 
6 designations and maintain the confidentiality of documents exchanged during the 
7 expedited discovery phase of the instant suit.  
8
On January 14, 2022, this Court issued an Order granting leave to take early 
9 discovery (Dkt. 10).  Since then, Plaintiff has obtained evidence providing for the 
10 identities of unnamed or alias “Doe” Defendants in this action, and anticipates 
11 obtaining more information as subpoenaed parties continue to provide responsive 
12 documents. By this Application, Plaintiff seeks to have the proposed Protective 
13 Order attached as Exhibit 1 to this Application entered by the Court in order to 
14 protect the confidentiality of information obtained through early discovery.  
15
This Application is based upon this Notice, the attached Memorandum of 
16 Points and Authorities, the Complaint in this action, and such other and further oral 
17 or documentary evidence and legal memoranda as may be presented at or before 
18 any hearing on this Application.  This Application is made on an ex parte basis in 
19 order to ensure that the Protective Order is entered (and, consequently, subpoena 
20 responses obtained and pleadings amended) on a timely basis.

MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND AUTHORITIES 
2
On January 13, 2022, Activision filed an Ex Parte Application (Dkt. 9) 
3 seeking leave to take circumscribed early discovery from various third party 
4 service providers that Defendants used to commit their illegal activities. This Court 
5 granted the application on January 14, 2022, and shortly thereafter Activision 
6 served the subpoenas. 
7
In the time since the subpoenas were served, certain of the third parties have 
8 sought to designate the documents they have produced as “Confidential” and/or 
9 have requested entry of a protective order, so as to facilitate the production of those 
10 documents while also protecting their own rights. 
11
Accordingly, by this Application, Activision seeks entry of the attached 
12 Protective Order, which is substantially the same as the Non-Attorneys’ Eyes Only 
13 Form Protective Order available at Magistrate Judge Chooljian’s website 
14 (https://www.cacd.uscourts.gov/honorable-jacqueline-chooljian).  Good cause 
15 exists for the entry of the Protective Order, as such an Order is necessary to clarify 
16 the parameters of “Confidential” designations from certain subpoenaed parties.  
17 Moreover, Activision cannot file a Stipulated Protective Order, as no other parties 
18 have yet appeared in this action. 
19
Accordingly, for the foregoing reasons Activision seeks to have the 
20 Protective Order attached as Exhibit 1 entered in the instant suit.

A. PURPOSES AND LIMITATIONS 
2
As Plaintiff Activision Publishing, Inc. (“Plaintiff”) has represented, discovery 
3 in this action is likely to involve production of confidential, proprietary, or private 
4 information for which special protection from public disclosure and from use for any 
5 purpose other than prosecuting this litigation may be warranted.  Accordingly, this 
6 Court  enters  the  following  Protective  Order.    This  Order  does  not  confer  blanket 
7 protections on all disclosures or responses to discovery.  The protection it affords from 
8 public disclosure and use extends only to the limited information or items that are 
9 entitled to confidential treatment under the applicable legal principles.  Further, as set 
10 forth in Section 12.3, below, this Protective Order does not entitle the parties to file 
11 confidential information under seal.  Rather, when the parties seek permission from 
12 the court to file material under seal, the parties must comply with Civil Local Rule 
13 79-5  and  with  any  pertinent  orders  of  the  assigned  District  Judge  and  Magistrate 
14 Judge. 
15
B. GOOD CAUSE STATEMENT 
16
In light of the nature of the claims and allegations in this case and Plaintiff’s 
17 representation that discovery in this case will involve the production of confidential 
18 records, and in order to expedite the flow of information, to facilitate the prompt 
19 resolution of disputes over confidentiality of discovery materials, to adequately 
20 protect information the parties are entitled to keep confidential, to ensure that the 
21 parties are permitted reasonable necessary uses of such material in connection with 
22 this action, to address their handling of such material at the end of the litigation, and 
23 to serve the ends of justice, a protective order for such information is justified in this 
24 matter.  It is Plaintiff’s intent that any information/documents shall not be 
25 designated as confidential without a good faith belief that such 
26 information/documents have been maintained in a confidential, non-public manner, 
27 and that there is good cause or a compelling reason why it should not be part of the 
28 public record of this case.
