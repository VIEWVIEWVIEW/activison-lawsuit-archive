NOTICE OF EX PARTE APPLICATION 
2
PLEASE TAKE NOTICE that Plaintiff Activision Publishing, Inc. 
3 (“Plaintiff” or “Activision”) hereby applies ex parte pursuant to Federal Rule of 
4 Civil Procedure 26(d) for leave to serve a Subpoena upon Coinbase, Inc. for the 
5 purpose of identifying “Doe” Defendants in this action. 
6
Good cause exists for leave to serve the requested subpoena.  Specifically, 
7 on January 14, 2022, this Court issued an Order granting leave to take early 
8 discovery (Dkt. 10), including to issue a subpoena to Coinbase Global, Inc. 
9 (“Coinbase Global.”)  However, Coinbase Global has claimed that the information 
10 requested is in the possession of its affiliate, Coinbase, Inc.  Thus, Coinbase Global 
11 has refused to provide responsive information, and has demanded that Activision 
12 issue a new subpoena to Coinbase, Inc.  By this Application, Plaintiff seeks leave 
13 to serve this additional subpoena.  A copy of the proposed Subpoena is attached as 
14 Exhibit 1 to this Motion.  The new subpoena is nearly identical to the one served 
15 on Coinbase Global, but one request has been narrowed and clarified. 
16
This Application is based upon this Notice, the attached Memorandum of 
17 Points and Authorities, the Complaint in this action, the original Ex Parte 
18 Application (Dkt. 9) and the Court’s Order granting the Ex Parte Application (Dkt. 
19 10), and such other and further oral or documentary evidence and legal memoranda 
20 as may be presented at or before any hearing on this Application.  This Application 
21 is made on an ex parte basis in order to ensure that the information is obtained 
22 (and, if necessary, any pleadings amended) on a timely basis.

MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND AUTHORITIES 
2
This is an action for violation of the anti-circumvention provisions of the 
3 Digital Millennium Copyright Act (“DMCA”), 17 U.S.C. § 1201, and for 
4 intentional interference with contract, arising from Defendants’ development, 
5 marketing, distribution, and sale of a malicious software product known as 
6 “EngineOwning” (“EO”).  The EO software enables players to cheat in the popular 
7 video game franchise Call of Duty.   
8
On January 14, 2022, this Court issued an Order granting an Application 
9 (Dkt. 9) by Plaintiff Activision Publishing, Inc. (“Plaintiff” or “Activision”) for 
10 leave to take limited early discovery in the form of fifteen subpoenas to third party 
11 service providers (Dkt. 10).  In that application, Activision explained this 
12 discovery would either identify, or lead to the discovery of, a group of “Doe” 
13 Defendants as yet unnamed in this case.  Among the subpoenas that the Court 
14 authorized Activision to serve was a subpoena to Coinbase Global, Inc. (“Coinbase 
15 Global”), a payment processor that provided services to EO and its agents or 
16 employees. 
17
On January 19, 2022, Activision served the Coinbase Global subpoena via 
18 its agent for service of process.  On February 15, 2022, Coinbase Global (via its 
19 outside counsel) served objections to the subpoena, and refused to produce any 
20 documents.  Specifically, in response to each of the requests in the subpoena, 
21 Coinbase Global provided the same exact same form objections, which included
