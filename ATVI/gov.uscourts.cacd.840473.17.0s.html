NOTICE OF EX PARTE APPLICATION 
2
PLEASE TAKE NOTICE that Plaintiff Activision Publishing, Inc. 
3 (“Plaintiff”) hereby applies ex parte pursuant to Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 
4 26(d) for the Court to enter a protective order (the “Protective Order”) in the 
5 above-captioned case in order to clarify the parameters of confidentiality 
6 designations and maintain the confidentiality of documents exchanged during the 
7 expedited discovery phase of the instant suit.  
8
On January 14, 2022, this Court issued an Order granting leave to take early 
9 discovery (Dkt. 10).  Since then, Plaintiff has obtained evidence providing for the 
10 identities of unnamed or alias “Doe” Defendants in this action, and anticipates 
11 obtaining more information as subpoenaed parties continue to provide responsive 
12 documents.   
13
On March 21, 2022, at Plaintiff’s request, the Court issued a Protective 
14 Order in order to protect the confidentiality of information obtained through early 
15 discovery (Dkt. 16).  However, after the issuance of the Protective Order, certain 
16 third parties advised Plaintiff that certain materials to be produced are highly 
17 sensitive and proprietary and must be designated as “Highly Confidential.”  These 
18 parties thus requested that the Protective Order be amended to include a second 
19 “Attorneys’ Eyes Only” tier of protection.  By this Application, Plaintiff seeks to 
20 have the proposed Amended Protective Order attached as Exhibit 1 to this 
21 Application entered by the Court in order to protect the confidentiality of 
22 information obtained through early discovery. The proposed Amended Protective 
23 Order complies with this Court’s model protective order and is largely identical to 
24 the prior Protective Order, except that it includes “Attorneys’ Eyes Only” 
25 provisions.

MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND AUTHORITIES 
2
On January 13, 2022, Activision filed an Ex Parte Application (Dkt. 9) 
3 seeking leave to take circumscribed early discovery from various third party 
4 service providers that Defendants used to commit their illegal activities. This Court 
5 granted the application on January 14, 2022, and shortly thereafter Activision 
6 served the subpoenas.  In the time since the subpoenas were served, certain of the 
7 third parties have sought to designate the documents they have produced as 
8 “Confidential” and/or have requested entry of a protective order, so as to facilitate 
9 the production of those documents while also protecting their own rights. 
10
On March 21, 2022, at Plaintiff’s request, the Court issued a Protective 
11 Order in order to protect the confidentiality of information obtained through early 
12 discovery (Dkt. 16).  However, after the issuance of the Protective Order, certain 
13 third parties advised Plaintiff that some specific materials they intend to produce 
14 are highly sensitive and proprietary, and must be subject to a “Highly 
15 Confidential” level of protection.  These parties thus requested that the Protective 
16 Order be amended to include a second “Attorneys’ Eyes Only” tier of protection.   
17
Accordingly, by this Application, Activision seeks entry of the attached 
18 Amended Protective Order, which is substantially the same as the Attorneys’ Eyes 
19 Only Form Protective Order available at Magistrate Judge Chooljian’s website 
20 (https://www.cacd.uscourts.gov/honorable-jacqueline-chooljian).  Additionally, 
21 except for the addition of an “Attorneys’ Eyes Only” tier, the Amended Protective 
22 Order is the same as the first Protective Order.  
23
Good cause exists for the entry of the Protective Order, as such an Order is 
24 necessary to clarify the parameters of “Confidential” and “Highly Confidential” 
25 designations from certain subpoenaed parties.  Moreover, Activision cannot file a 
26 Stipulated Protective Order, as no other parties have yet appeared in this action. 
27
Accordingly, for the foregoing reasons Activision seeks to have the 
Mitchell 
28 Amended Protective Order attached as Exhibit 1 entered in the instant suit.
